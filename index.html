💡 Pricing Pro Tip: Debris Type Matters!

Adjust your load price based on disposal costs:

Higher Price: Wood, construction debris, concrete, dirt → Must go to landfill (high dump fees)

Lower Price: Furniture, appliances, household items → Can be recycled or donated (low/no dump fees)

Mixed Loads: Combination of both → Price in the middle

This strategy helps you win more jobs while protecting margins. You avoid under-bidding landfill jobs and over-bidding donation jobs!

📦 Multiple Loads Pro Tip

When pricing jobs requiring multiple full loads, it's smart to offer a per-load discount (10-15%). Example: Instead of $600/load × 3 = $1,800, offer 10% off = $1,620. Customer saves $180, you lock in a $1,600+ job!

💡 Lawn Care Pro Tips

  • Always see the yard before quoting — photos lie. Overgrown yards take 3x longer.
  • Bundle services to increase ticket size. A mow + edge + blow + trim is worth more than individual quotes.
  • Route your jobs geographically — driving between jobs is your biggest hidden cost.
  • Recurring contracts: Weekly or biweekly mowing contracts are more predictable income than one-time jobs. Offer a small discount for recurring bookings to lock customers in.

💡 Driveway Tip: Concrete driveways with oil stains may need degreaser ($10–20 extra cost). Charge accordingly — stained driveways take longer and use more chemical.

💡 House Wash Tip: Most house washes are soft wash (low pressure + chemical), not high pressure. Soft wash uses more chemical but protects the siding. Factor in soap cost and rinse time.

Average deck is 200–400 sq ft

💡 Deck Tip: Wood decks need lower pressure than concrete to avoid damage. Add an upsell for sealing/staining after washing — that's a $300–800 add-on job you can offer on the spot.

Measure total linear feet of fence to be washed

💡 Fence Tip: Wood fences take longer and need gentler pressure. Vinyl fences are faster. Price wood 20–30% higher than vinyl for the same linear footage.

💡 Roof Soft Wash Tip: Never use high pressure on roofs — you'll void warranties and blow off shingles. Soft wash is chemical-heavy (sodium hypochlorite + surfactant). Chemical cost can be $30–80 per job — price accordingly.

💡 Commercial Tip: Commercial jobs often require a certificate of insurance (COI). If you have one, charge a premium — many competitors can't bid commercial jobs without it.

💡 Pressure Washing Pro Tips

  • Pressure washing has some of the highest margins in home services — your main costs are time, chemical, and fuel. Protect those margins.
  • Always upsell. If you're already washing the siding, offer to do the driveway too. Incremental jobs on the same stop are nearly pure profit.
  • Take before & after photos of every job — these are your best marketing material for social media.
  • Surface cleaner attachment: Invest in a surface cleaner for flat work (driveways, patios). It cuts your time in half and leaves a more professional result.
